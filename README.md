# CS250-Sprint-Review
A retrospective about what I've learned regarding the Agile Method.


**How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?**

To interpret user needs and implement them, you start by conducting market research. This could be looking at general trends, consulting with stakeholders in your field, or conducting studies on focus groups. Then, you look at what features users either explicitly or implicitly want and implement them as concrete actions users can take. A user story represents these concrete actions by creating types of users, what they want to do, and a step A-to-B explanation of how to do it with clear pass/fail measures.


How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

Developing programs should be done incrementally, task-to-task, with a testbench that accumulates past contributions and ensures the program properly works. I hope to incorporate the concept of Sprints and Daily Scrums into future development work as it makes work more about day-to-day tasks rather than looming deadlines and design without pivots.


What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member requires proper communication, an egalitarian and respectful approach to others, and the technical skills to develop, test, or manage other people well. This requires both experience in development and interacting gracefully with others.
